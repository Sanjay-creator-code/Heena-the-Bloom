✦ Meet the Artist

Anjali Soni

✦

With over 5 years of dedicated practice, Anjali Soni has transformed mehndi from a craft into a deeply personal form of expression. Based in Jaora, Madhya Pradesh, she brings the ancient art of henna to life with extraordinary detail and care.

Her designs weave together classical Indian motifs — peacocks, lotus blooms, paisleys, and fine geometric patterns — with a modern elegance that suits every celebration. From intimate family gatherings to grand bridal ceremonies, Anjali's work adorns the hands and hearts of those who trust her art.

✦ Her Journey

The Art of Anjali

✦

Anjali Soni's love for mehndi began not in a classroom but in the warmth of her family's celebrations. As a young girl in Jaora, she watched in awe as patterns bloomed across her mother's and grandmother's hands before every puja and wedding. That childhood wonder never left her — it deepened into a lifelong calling.

From Tradition to Mastery

Over the past 5+ years, Anjali has refined her craft through relentless practice, learning from the finest traditional motifs of Rajasthan and Madhya Pradesh while incorporating contemporary aesthetics that speak to modern sensibilities. Her signature style blends fine-line florals, dense mandala patterns, and graceful peacocks with storytelling elements that make each design unique to its wearer.

She uses only the finest quality henna — natural, fresh, and prepared with care — ensuring deep, rich color that lasts and skin that feels nurtured rather than irritated.

Serving the Community

Having served 100+ satisfied clients across Jaora and the surrounding regions of Madhya Pradesh, Anjali has become a trusted name for families planning their most precious occasions. Brides seek her months in advance; mothers request her for their daughters' engagements; friends bring her along to baby showers as a cherished gift.

Nurturing New Talent

Anjali believes deeply that the art of henna belongs to everyone. Beyond client work, she dedicates time to coaching and mentoring new artists — young women and aspiring mehndi practitioners who come to her to learn. She teaches not just technique but also the philosophy behind the art: patience, intention, and the sacred role of mehndi in Indian culture.

Her workshops have helped dozens of local artists begin their own journeys, and she takes quiet pride in seeing her students flourish at their own celebrations and events.
